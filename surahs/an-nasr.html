Surah An-Nasr Meaning and Lessons

Surah An-Nasr teaches believers how to respond to victory: praise Allah, glorify Him, and ask His forgiveness. This page gives the meaning in English and simple explanation.

Arabic Text

إِذَا جَاءَ نَصْرُ اللَّهِ وَالْفَتْحُ
وَرَأَيْتَ النَّاسَ يَدْخُلُونَ فِي دِينِ اللَّهِ أَفْوَاجًا
فَسَبِّحْ بِحَمْدِ رَبِّكَ وَاسْتَغْفِرْهُ إِنَّهُ كَانَ تَوَّابًا

English Translation

When the help of Allah and victory come, and people enter Islam in crowds, believers should glorify their Lord with praise and seek His forgiveness, because He accepts repentance.

Word-by-Word Meaning

Nasr means help or victory. Fath means opening or conquest. Istaghfirhu means ask Him for forgiveness.

Tafsir (Simple Explanation)

The surah teaches humility after success. When Allah gives victory, the believer does not become arrogant. He becomes more thankful, more humble, and more aware of Allah.

Benefits of Surah An-Nasr

  • It teaches the right attitude after success.
  • It reminds believers that help comes from Allah.
  • It combines gratitude with repentance.

Explanation for Kids

Surah An-Nasr teaches kids that when something good happens, they should thank Allah. Winning or succeeding should make us more humble, not more proud.
