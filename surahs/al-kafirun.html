Surah 109

Surah Al-Kafirun Meaning and Explanation

Surah Al-Kafirun clearly teaches that worship belongs only to Allah. This page explains its meaning in English, word-by-word notes, simple tafsir, and practical lessons.

Arabic Text

قُلْ يَا أَيُّهَا الْكَافِرُونَ
لَا أَعْبُدُ مَا تَعْبُدُونَ
وَلَا أَنتُمْ عَابِدُونَ مَا أَعْبُدُ
وَلَا أَنَا عَابِدٌ مَّا عَبَدتُّمْ
وَلَا أَنتُمْ عَابِدُونَ مَا أَعْبُدُ
لَكُمْ دِينُكُمْ وَلِيَ دِينِ

English Translation

This surah tells disbelievers that the Prophet does not worship what they worship, and they do not worship what he worships. It ends with a clear separation: your religion for you, and my religion for me.

Word-by-Word Meaning

Al-kafirun means the disbelievers. La a'budu means I do not worship. Din means religion or way of belief.

Tafsir (Simple Explanation)

The surah teaches respectful firmness. Islam does not mix worship of Allah with false worship. Faith stays clear when the believer stays sincere and does not compromise the truth.

Benefits of Surah Al-Kafirun

  • It strengthens sincerity in worship.
  • It teaches clarity without confusion.
  • It is a useful short surah for prayer and memorization.

Explanation for Kids

Surah Al-Kafirun teaches children that Muslims worship Allah alone. We can be polite to others, but we do not mix Allah's worship with false worship.
