Surah 114

Surah An-Nas Explanation for Kids (Meaning in English)

Surah An-Nas is a short surah of protection from bad whispers and harmful thoughts. This page explains the meaning in English, simple tafsir, benefits, and easy lessons for kids and families.

Arabic Text

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ
مَلِكِ النَّاسِ
إِلَٰهِ النَّاسِ
مِن شَرِّ الْوَسْوَاسِ الْخَنَّاسِ
الَّذِي يُوَسْوِسُ فِي صُدُورِ النَّاسِ
مِنَ الْجِنَّةِ وَالنَّاسِ

English Translation

Surah An-Nas tells believers to seek refuge in Allah, the Lord, King, and God of people, from the evil of whispers that enter hearts from jinn and human beings.

Word-by-Word Meaning

Rabb means Lord and caretaker. Malik means King. Ilah means the One worthy of worship. Waswas means whispering or evil suggestions.

Tafsir (Simple Explanation)

This surah closes the Quran with a practical dua. It teaches that inner battles are real and that the heart stays safe by turning to Allah. Repeating Allah's names in this surah reminds believers that He fully controls and protects them.

Benefits of Surah An-Nas

  • It is one of the easiest surahs children learn for daily protection.
  • It helps believers respond to fear, anxiety, and bad thoughts by remembering Allah.
  • It is often read together with Surah Al-Falaq and Surah Al-Ikhlas.

Explanation for Kids

Surah An-Nas teaches children that if scary thoughts or bad ideas come into the heart, they should ask Allah for help. Allah is stronger than every whisper and keeps people safe.
