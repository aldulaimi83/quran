حم ۝ تَنزِيلٌ مِّنَ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

Simple Meaning

Also known as Ha-Mim As-Sajdah, this surah speaks of the Quran as a detailed explanation, the stories of 'Ad and Thamud, and the day when people's own ears, eyes, and skin will testify against them.

Key Lessons

  • And among His signs are the night and the day, and the sun and moon — do not prostrate to the sun or moon, but prostrate to Allah who created them (41:37).
  • Those who say: Our Lord is Allah and then are steadfast — the angels descend upon them (41:30).
  • Repel evil with what is better — and the one between whom there was enmity will become as if a close friend (41:34).
