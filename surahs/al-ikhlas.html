Surah 112

Surah Al-Ikhlas Meaning in English (Full Tafsir & Benefits)

Surah Al-Ikhlas is one of the clearest and shortest chapters about tawhid, the oneness of Allah. This page gives the meaning in English, simple tafsir, benefits, and easy lessons for children and beginners.

Arabic Text

قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ
اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ
لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ
وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ

English Translation

Surah Al-Ikhlas teaches that Allah is One, completely perfect, and not dependent on anyone. He has no parent, no child, and nothing in creation is equal to Him.

Word-by-Word Meaning

Ahad means One and unique. As-Samad means the One everyone needs while He needs no one. Lam yalid wa lam yulad means He neither gives birth nor was born.

Tafsir (Simple Explanation)

This surah corrects false ideas about Allah and gives a pure statement of Islamic belief. It teaches that true worship begins with knowing who Allah is: perfect, eternal, unique, and above all weakness or human qualities.

Benefits of Surah Al-Ikhlas

  • It is short, easy to memorize, and often read in daily prayer.
  • It strengthens a Muslim's understanding of tawhid.
  • It is one of the best surahs to teach children when introducing belief in Allah.

Explanation for Kids

Surah Al-Ikhlas tells kids something very important: Allah is only One, He is not like people, and He is perfect. It helps children grow up with a clear and simple idea of who Allah is.
