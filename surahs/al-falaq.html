Surah 113

Surah Al-Falaq Benefits and Meaning in English

Surah Al-Falaq is one of the short surahs of protection. This page explains its Arabic text, English meaning, simple tafsir, benefits, and easy lessons for children and new readers.

Arabic Text

قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ الْفَلَقِ
مِن شَرِّ مَا خَلَقَ
وَمِن شَرِّ غَاسِقٍ إِذَا وَقَبَ
وَمِن شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ
وَمِن شَرِّ حَاسِدٍ إِذَا حَسَدَ

English Translation

Surah Al-Falaq asks Allah, the Lord of daybreak, to protect the believer from the harm of creation, darkness, envy, and hidden evil.

Word-by-Word Meaning

A'udhu means I seek protection. Rabb al-falaq means Lord of the breaking dawn. Hasid means the envious person whose jealousy causes harm.

Tafsir (Simple Explanation)

This surah trains the heart to run to Allah when fear appears. It teaches that visible and hidden dangers exist, but the believer responds by seeking Allah's care instead of panic.

Benefits of Surah Al-Falaq

  • It is commonly read for daily protection in the morning and evening.
  • It reminds believers that safety comes from Allah, not from superstition.
  • It is short and practical, making it easy for children to learn and use.

Explanation for Kids

Surah Al-Falaq teaches kids to ask Allah for safety when they feel scared, worried, or upset. It tells them Allah protects them from harm they can see and harm they cannot see.
