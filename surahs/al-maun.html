Surah 107

Surah Al-Ma'un Meaning and Explanation

Surah Al-Ma'un warns against fake religion that ignores orphans, neglects prayer, and refuses small acts of help. This page gives simple meaning, tafsir, and lessons.

Arabic Text

أَرَأَيْتَ الَّذِي يُكَذِّبُ بِالدِّينِ
فَذَٰلِكَ الَّذِي يَدُعُّ الْيَتِيمَ
وَلَا يَحُضُّ عَلَىٰ طَعَامِ الْمِسْكِينِ
فَوَيْلٌ لِّلْمُصَلِّينَ
الَّذِينَ هُمْ عَن صَلَاتِهِمْ سَاهُونَ
الَّذِينَ هُمْ يُرَاءُونَ
وَيَمْنَعُونَ الْمَاعُونَ

English Translation

This surah criticizes people who deny the reality of judgment by pushing away orphans, failing to care for the poor, praying carelessly, showing off, and refusing even small acts of help.

Word-by-Word Meaning

Yadu''u means he pushes away harshly. Yura'un means they show off. Al-ma'un means small everyday help or useful items.

Tafsir (Simple Explanation)

The surah teaches that worship is not only words. Real faith shows up in prayer, sincerity, mercy, and kindness to people in need.

Benefits of Surah Al-Ma'un

  • It reminds the heart that Islam includes social responsibility.
  • It warns against empty religious performance.
  • It teaches children that small acts of kindness matter.

Explanation for Kids

Surah Al-Ma'un teaches children to be kind, honest in prayer, and helpful to others. A good Muslim is not selfish and does not pretend to be good just for show.
